About Kanha

The Crown Jewel of Indian Tiger Reserves

Spread across more than 940 sq km of pristine central Indian forests in Madhya Pradesh, Kanha National Park is one of the oldest and most celebrated tiger reserves in Asia. Home to over 100 Royal Bengal Tigers, leopards, wild dogs, barasingha, and 300+ bird species, Kanha offers an unmatched wildlife experience for nature lovers, photographers, and adventure seekers. Curious about what lives here beyond the tiger? Read our wildlife of Kanha National Park field guide.

Whether you are planning your first jungle safari or returning for a deeper immersion into the wild, this guide covers the very best things to do in Kanha National Park — from sunrise jeep safaris in Mukki Zone to starlit evenings at our handpicked jungle resort. Top Activities

Unmissable Things to Do in Kanha National Park

From tiger-spotting jeep safaris to soul-soothing nature walks — your Kanha bucket list, fully covered.

1. Tiger Jeep Safari

The flagship experience. Ride through Sal forests with a trained naturalist in an open jeep — your best chance of spotting a Royal Bengal Tiger in its natural habitat. Book the curated Kanha tiger safari tour package for permit-handled access.

2. Nature & Forest Walks

Explore Kanha's buffer zone on foot with an experienced forest guide. Discover medicinal plants, butterflies, and tracks that vehicles simply can't reach. Most guided walks start near the gate — see our best resorts near Khatia Gate to base yourself within walking distance.

3. Birdwatching Tours

With 300+ avian species, Kanha is a paradise for birders. Look out for the Indian Pitta, Crested Serpent Eagle, and the Paradise Flycatcher. Our naturalists cover them all in the wildlife of Kanha National Park field guide.

4. Wildlife Photography Safaris

Customised photography jeeps, gyro-stabilised mounts and expert spotters — built for serious shutterbugs chasing the perfect tiger frame. Pair it with the Kanha jungle safari package for off-road buffer access.

5. Barasingha Conservation Visit

Kanha is the only place in the world where the hard-ground Barasingha (swamp deer) survives. Visit the conservation area to see this success story up close — and read the full recovery arc on our wildlife of Kanha National Park page.

6. Resort Stay & Bonfire Nights

End your day at a handpicked jungle resort — private cottages, candlelit dinners, tribal dance evenings and starlit bonfires under a Sal canopy. Stay at our best resort in Kanha National Park for the full experience.

7. Tribal Village Tourism

Visit Gond and Baiga villages on Kanha's fringes — meet local artisans, taste regional cuisine and experience the cultural roots of the forest. Need a base camp for village tours? See where to stay near Kanha National Park.

8. Night Safari in Buffer Zone

Spot civets, flying squirrels, hyenas and nightjars on guided nocturnal drives through Kanha's buffer — a thrilling off-the-beaten-path activity. Permit rules change every season; our Kanha safari booking guide covers the latest buffer-zone timings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Want to Know Before Booking

What are the best things to do in Kanha National Park?

Top things to do include jeep safaris (Mukki, Kisli and Sarhi zones), nature walks, birdwatching tours, wildlife photography safaris, Barasingha conservation visits, tribal village tours and luxurious jungle resort stays.

How many days are ideal for a Kanha trip?

3 to 4 days is ideal for a focused Kanha experience. If you wish to combine with Pench or Bandhavgarh, 6 to 7 days is recommended for a relaxed pace.

What is the best time to visit Kanha?

The park is open from October to June. November to February offers pleasant weather, while March to May offers the highest tiger-sighting chances as animals gather near water sources. See the full month-by-month guide on our best time to visit Kanha National Park page.

Are children allowed on jungle safaris?

Yes. Children above 5 years are allowed on jeep safaris accompanied by an adult. We also offer family-friendly resorts with kids' zones and curated short safaris.

Do you handle safari permits and zone allocation?

Absolutely. We manage 100% of safari permits, zone allocation, vehicle arrangement and naturalist accompaniment — you simply arrive and enjoy.

How far is Kanha from the nearest airport / station?

Jabalpur Airport (JLR) is about 160 km away (3.5 hours). Gondia & Nagpur are also popular rail/air options with private transfers arranged by our team. Full route options on our how to reach Kanha page.

Is photography allowed inside the park?

Yes, photography is encouraged. For serious photographers, we offer dedicated photography jeeps, gyro mounts and spotter-led drives to maximise frame quality. Pair it with the Kanha jungle safari package.

What is the cancellation policy?

Cancellations made 30 days before travel receive a full refund minus processing fees. Park permits, once issued, are non-transferable as per forest department rules. Full terms on our Kanha cancellation policy page.
